Matrix Loading — Two-Pass Sparse Reader

Why two passes? What does each pass do? How does sparsity reduce cost?

▶ Two-Pass Loading — Step by Step Step 1 of 5

Sparsity Visualisation

Only ~3–8% of (cell, locus) pairs have any reads. The sparse CellData structure stores only these non-zero entries — giving a 10–100× speedup over a dense implementation.

N=60 cells × L=120 loci — each column = one locus, each row = one cell · teal = covered
■ Non-zero (alt or ref reads) ■ Zero coverage
Key design insight
D ≈ 10–200 covered loci per cell vs L ≈ 500–20,000 total QC-passing loci. The sparse CellData struct stores only covered loci — giving a 10–100× speedup over a dense O(N·L·K) implementation with identical mathematical results.

§ 8

E-step Deep Dive — Posterior Computation

The E-step is the heart of EM. For every cell, it asks: "given the current θ estimates, what is the probability this cell belongs to each cluster?"

▶ E-step Formula — Revealed Term by Term Step 1 of 5
Hot path
binomial_loss_with_min_index is called N × S × I × (R/T) times per thread. For N=5000, S=9, I=15, R=100, T=8: ≈ 84 million calls, each looping over D≈50 loci × K=4 clusters. This single function accounts for >80% of total runtime.

K-Harmonic Means (KHM) Algorithm

KHM replaces the EM softmax with a harmonic-mean membership function that is more robust to poor initialisations — at the cost of formal convergence guarantees.

Peak memory estimate
For N=5000, D=100, K=4, L=8000, T=8, R=100:
CellData ≈ 5000×100×12B = 6 MB · θ per thread ≈ 4×8000×4B = 128 KB × 8 = 1 MB · log_probs ≈ 5000×4×4B = 80 KB × 8 = 640 KB · Total ≈ ~10 MB — well within RAM bounds for typical datasets.

Bottom line
The v3.0.0 rewrite is architecturally sound. The clustering loop is the correct bottleneck. The sparse CellData design already captures the most important algorithmic optimisation. Future gains come from hardware-level SIMD, not from algorithmic redesign.
